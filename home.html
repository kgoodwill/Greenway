Welcome to the Binghamton Greenway

Binghamton Greenway

What is the purpose of the Greenway?

There are inadequate pedestrian and bicycle accomodations within the proposed project limits. This could be solved by the building of the Greater Binghamton Greenway. The objectives of this project segment include the following:

  • Provide pedestrian and bicycle communter access
  • Provide safe, accessible pedestrian and bicycle accomodations between Binghamton University, University Plaza, and Downtown Binghamton
  • Provide recreational opportunities for residents and visitors in the project area
What is the scope of the project?

This projects consists of three segments:

  • South Washington Street to MacArthur Park and School
  • MacArthur Park and School to Clubhouse Road
  • Clubhouse Road to Binghamton University

Segment One: South Washington Street to MacArthur School and Park

There are 3 options for this section of the Greenway

Overpass

This option proposes an overpass that will allow the Greenway to proceed over Vestal Parkway.

Pros: Cons:
Separates trail users from motor vechicle traffic Grade may be difficult for some trail users
Highly Visible Significantly more expensive to build than any other alternative
Identifies trail location The most cost-effective prefabricated bridge options are not the most aesthetic
Enhances safety Expensive to maintain over the long run
No affect on motor vehicle traffic
Overall Estimate: $2.75 million
Underpass

This option proposes an underpass that will allow the Greenway to pass under Vestal Parkway.


Pros:

  • Trail is closer to the river than other alternatives
  • Trail is buffered from traffic State Route 434
  • Aesthetics of site adjacent to river
  • It will not affect traffic on State Route 434

Cons:

  • Involvement with State Route 434 embankment
  • Grade on the south approach may be difficult for some trail users
  • Lower visibility than other alternatives
  • Security and vandalism concerns
  • Trail will be "hidden" from State Route 434
  • Could incur maintanence costs not associated with other issues

Overall Estimate: $1.1 million
At Grade

This option proposes an at grade road crossing for the Greenway to cross State Route 434

Pros:

  • Lowest construction and maintenance cost
  • No added security concerns
  • No steep approach or descent

Cons:

  • Least safe for trail users
  • It will affect traffic on State Route 434
  • Doesn't completely separate trail users from motor vechicles creating a conflict point
  • State Route 434 is a high speed facility
  • Snow removal on median crossover may be a problem
Overall Estimate: $0.8 million
