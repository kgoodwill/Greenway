FAQs

Q: What is the Binghamton Bike Share? A: The Binghamton Bike Share is a free service that allows any student or faculty member to borrow a bike.

Q: How much does it cost to use the bike share? A: The bike share is a free program for all users. Fees will be charged to anyone who damages the system or does not abide by our rules.

Q: How do I register for the program? A: In order to register for the bike share, you can sign up on our website or via Facebook. You can also register directly at the Information Desk located in the Old Union.

Q: How do I take out a bike? A: Go to the Old Union Information Desk and you will be given a key that will unlock one of our bikes.

Q: Where can I find the bikes? A: Our bike rack is located under the engineering building near the library. The bikes available for use have a bright green flag with a number on the flag.

Q: How long may I borrow a bike for? A: You may borrow a Bike Share bike for up to 4 hours before returning the bike and key.

Q: What do I do if I get a flat tire while riding a bike? A: Return the bike as soon as possible and e-mail us at BinghamtonBikeShare@gmail.com. Once you return it, feel free to borrow another bike.

Q: Do I have to wear a helmet while riding a Bike Share bike? A: We offer helmets to each user and we strongly recommend the use for your general safety. However, NYS law does not require that helmets be worn

Q: Can my friends use the bikes even if they don’t go to Binghamton? A: Yes, you can sign out bikes for friends with your own ID card. However, the responsibility for the bikes is on whoever rents out the bikes, so make sure they don’t break the bikes or the rules!

Q: What happens if I do not return the bike on time? A: You will be charged a fee of $5 per hour for the first two hours you are late. After, you will be charged $10 per hour. If you keep the bike overnight, you will be charged an additional $25.

Q: What happens if I damage the bike? A: Any damage that you cause to a bike will be charged to you through Student Accounts based on how much maintenance will be required.

Q: When is the program open? A: Bike Share operates daily from 12-8:00 PM. The program will be open for the spring and the fall. The program stops running when the first significant snowfall occurs. For more updates on the Bike Share, like our Facebook page!

Q: When will you get more racks? A: As soon as we renew our subscription to Playboy.

Q: The bike seat is too high/low for me. What do I do A: Adjust the bike seat by opening the quick-release lever, raising or lowering the seat post, and reclosing the lever. We will add informative videos to our website.

Q: Can I donate my own bike to the program? A: Yes! If you wish to donate your bike, please contact us and we can easily implement your bike into the system.

Q: Am I allowed to take the bike off campus? A: Yes, as long as the bike is returned on time and proper locking techniques are being used. It is your responsibility to bring the bike back in working order.

Q: How do I lock my bike? A: Attached to each bike is a lock for your convenience. There is a video on our website on how to use the lock.

Q: I have my own bike that needs repair. Where can I get help? A: Bike Share runs a repair shop in the basement of the Old Union, next to the Undergrounds.

Q: How do I return my bike? A: Lock the rear tire of the bike to our bike rack in front of the Engineering Building. Return the key at the Old Union desk and sign out. Record any damage or reviews on the bike or the system. If you are the last person to use the bike and there are damages, you may be charged.

Q: How can I join the maintenance team? A: If you are interested in joining our team, Outdoor Pursuits is offering a class (CEO 395) to teach bike share members how to repair bikes. Contact us for more information.

Q: Am I allowed to ride a bike while under the influence? A: No. It is unsafe and illegal to ride while under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

Q: How do I become part of the Binghamton Bike Share? A: We hold our meetings every Monday! Contact us for times!

Q: How can I contact the Binghamton Bike Share? A: You can contact us via our Facebook page or email us at BinghamtonBikeShare@gmail.com.

Also, please see our website for more information.
